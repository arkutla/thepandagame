Information about this study

Thank you for considering taking part in this research. If you have any questions arising from the information on this page or explanation already given to you, please contact the researcher before you decide whether to join in. You may want to save or print this page to keep for your records.

  • The approximate duration of this study is 5 minutes.
  • Participation in this study is entirely anonymous.
  • You are free to end the study at any time. However, please note that we are only able to pay you if you complete the whole study.
  • In addition to payment for your time, you could gain a bonus payment based on your performance of up to £2. Please read the instructions carefully to find out how to gain the bonus.

Eligibility

  • You are eligible to take part if you are at leat 18 years of age.
  • The study must be completed on a desktop or laptop. If you are currently using a mobile device, please switch to a laptop or desktop computer before starting this study.

What happens if I take part?

In this study you will play a game where the goal is to feed a panda and make it happy again. You will see a playing field and be able to move using the arrow keys on your keyboard. Along the way, you will be able to collect honey for the panda. Based on how well you do in the game, you will get bonus payments. Full instructions will be given at the start of the study. We are interested in your performance in the study and how you make choices based on the information you get from previous players. There is no time limit in this study.

Should I take part in this study?

It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you have any questions arising from the Information Section or explanation already given to you, please ask the researcher before you decide whether to participate. You can withdraw at any time without giving a reason. If you decide to withdraw during the study, the data you have provided up that point will be destroyed. As the data collected is anonymous, we may not be able to delete your data after you have completed the study.

What if something goes wrong?

If you wish to raise a complaint about any aspect of this study, you should contact the Principal Researcher in the first instance. If you feel your complaint has not been handled to your satisfaction you can contact the Chair of the UCL Research Ethics Committee at ethics@ucl.ac.uk.

What will happen to the results of the research project?

The results of the research will be presented in a MSc thesis by the researcher, and potentially published in an article or other formats. If you'd like to receive a summary of the findings, you can contact the researcher. Anonymous data collected during the study may be made publically available on platforms such as the Open Science Framework (osf.io) and GitHub (github.com). This data will not include information which would allow others to identify you.
